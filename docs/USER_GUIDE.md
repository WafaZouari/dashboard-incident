# 📖 User Guide: How to use the Dashboard

Welcome to the Petrochemical Incident Dashboard! This guide explains how to navigate the app and use its key features.

---

## 📊 1. The Dashboard (Overview)
When you first log in, you'll see the **Safety Overview**.
- **Metrics at a Glance**: Total incidents, high-potential risks, and open investigations.
- **Trend Charts**: Visual graphs showing if incidents are increasing or decreasing over time.
- **Incident List**: A table of the most recent events that need attention.

## 📈 2. Analytics Page
The Analytics page is designed for managers and safety officers.
- **Heatmaps**: Identify which plant units (e.g., "Refinery Unit A") have the most frequent issues.
- **Severity Breakdown**: See the ratio of "Minor Injuries" vs. "High Potential" events.
- **Filtering**: Use the date and category filters to drill down into specific data points.

## 📝 3. Managing Incidents
### Reporting an Incident
1. Click on **"Report New Incident"**.
2. Fill in the title, date, and location.
3. Provide a clear description.
4. **AI Tip**: The AI will automatically read your description and suggest a "Severity Level" and "Category" to save you time.

### Investigations
Every major incident can have an investigation attached to it. Here, investigators can log:
- Root Cause Analysis.
- Findings and Recommendations.
- **Action Items**: Assign specific tasks to staff members (e.g., "Fix valve in Unit B") and track their completion.

## 🤖 4. AI Features
The dashboard uses built-in Artificial Intelligence to help you work faster:
- **Summarization**: The AI can condense long incident reports into a 2-sentence executive summary.
- **Risk Assessment**: It helps predict if a small "Near Miss" has the potential to become a major event.
- **Smart Search**: Find past incidents similar to the one you are currently investigating.

---

## 💡 Best Practices
- **Be Descriptive**: The AI works best when you provide detailed incident descriptions.
- **Close the Loop**: Always mark Action Items as "Completed" once the physical work is done to keep the metrics accurate.
- **Check Filters**: If you don't see an incident you just added, double-check your date filters!
